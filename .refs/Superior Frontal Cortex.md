## What is Brain SFC?

"Brain SFC" typically refers to two core concepts in neuroscience and medical research:

### 1. Anatomical Concept: Superior Frontal Cortex

This is the most common reference, located at the uppermost part of the frontal lobe.

- **Main Functions**: Deeply involved in **Executive Functions**, such as self-monitoring, working memory, organization, and planning.
- **Roles of Different Subdivisions**:
     - **Left SFC**: Focuses on processing, monitoring, and manipulating high-level working memory.
     - **Right SFC**: Closely related to **Impulse Control**, regulating inhibitory functions and motor urgency.
     - **Medial SFC** (including Supplementary Motor Area, SMA): Plays a key role in **Motor Control** and cognitive control.
- **Clinical Significance**: Dysfunction in this area can lead to abnormal emotional regulation (e.g., excessive anxiety) or working memory deficits.

### 2. Neuroscience Research Concept: Structural-Functional Coupling (SFC)

In brain network research and neuroimaging (e.g., fMRI and dMRI), SFC refers to the **degree of consistency** between the brain's physical structure (white matter fiber connections) and its functional activity (synchrony between brain regions).

- **Dynamicity**: The degree of SFC coupling fluctuates over time, known as "SFC temporal variability," reflecting the brain's ability to integrate complex spatiotemporal features.
- **Technical Applications**: Researchers have developed AI models like **SFC-GAN** to simulate and predict between Structural Connectivity (SC) and Functional Connectivity (FC).

**Note**: A clinically common abbreviation that might be confused is **CSF** (Cerebrospinal Fluid), the colorless liquid surrounding the brain and spinal cord that provides cushioning.

## Relationship Between the Cerebral Cortex and the Prefrontal Cortex

Simply put, the **Prefrontal Cortex (PFC) is the most "advanced" subdivision of the Cerebral Cortex**.

If you compare the Cerebral Cortex to a massive corporation, the **Prefrontal Cortex is the "CEO Office"** of that company.

### 1. Inclusion: Whole and Part

- **Cerebral Cortex (The Whole)**: A layer of grey matter, about 2-4 mm thick, covering the surface of the cerebral hemispheres. It is divided into four main lobes: Frontal, Parietal, Temporal, and Occipital.
- **Prefrontal Cortex (The Part)**: Located in the **anterior part of the Frontal Lobe**. it is the latest-developing and most functionally complex area in the human brain, accounting for nearly 30% of the total area of the human cerebral cortex (compared to only 3% in cats).

### 2. Functional Localization: Execution and Integration

While different parts of the cortex have specific roles, the PFC acts as the commander:

| Region                      | Primary Function                                                         | Role                            |
| :-------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------ |
| **Visual/Auditory Cortex**  | Receives and processes basic sensory signals                             | Front-line Staff                |
| **Motor Cortex**            | Controls muscle activity and bodily movement                             | Production Department           |
| **Prefrontal Cortex (PFC)** | **Decision-making, planning, impulse inhibition, logic, working memory** | **Decision-making Layer (CEO)** |

### 3. How does the PFC "Manage" the Cortex?

The PFC communicates with other cortical regions through complex neural circuits:

- **Top-down Control**: For example, when you focus on someone's voice at a noisy party, the PFC commands the "Auditory Cortex" to filter out noise and focus on that specific sound.
- **Braking Function**: When your primitive cortex (e.g., limbic system) produces an impulse to get angry, the PFC evaluates the consequences and attempts to "brake," keeping you calm.
- **Information Integration**: It collects all data from sensory cortices, combines it with past experience, and formulates a plan for future action.

**Note on SFC (Superior Frontal Cortex) Connection**:
The **SFC (Superior Frontal Cortex)** mentioned earlier is an important component within the **Prefrontal Cortex**. Located at the uppermost layer of the PFC, it is primarily responsible for high-level cognitive processing and self-awareness.

> **Tip**: The PFC is very fragile. Lack of sleep, chronic stress, or alcohol will first weaken PFC function, leading to slowed reactions, loss of emotional control, or difficulty concentrating.
